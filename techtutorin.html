How to solve this differential equation: dy/dx + 2y = x^2e^(-2x)?

Asked 2 days ago

Answered
Dr. Jane Cooper
Answered 1 day ago

To solve the differential equation dy/dx + 2y = x^2e^(-2x), we'll use the integrating factor method.

Step 1: Identify the standard form. This equation is already in the standard form dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x), where P(x) = 2 and Q(x) = x^2e^(-2x).

Step 2: Find the integrating factor μ(x) = e^(∫P(x)dx) = e^(∫2dx) = e^(2x).

Step 3: Multiply both sides by the integrating factor:

e^(2x) * (dy/dx + 2y) = e^(2x) * x^2e^(-2x)
e^(2x) * dy/dx + 2e^(2x) * y = x^2

Step 4: Recognize that the left side equals d/dx(e^(2x) * y):
d/dx(e^(2x) * y) = x^2

Step 5: Integrate both sides:
e^(2x) * y = ∫x^2 dx = x^3/3 + C

Step 6: Solve for y:
y = e^(-2x) * (x^3/3 + C)

Therefore, the general solution is y = (x^3/3 + C)e^(-2x), where C is the constant of integration.

What is the time complexity of quicksort algorithm?

Asked 3 days ago

Answered
Robert Johnson
Answered 2 days ago

The time complexity of the quicksort algorithm varies depending on the scenario:

  • Best case: O(n log n) - This occurs when the pivot chosen divides the array into roughly equal halves each time.
  • Average case: O(n log n) - This is the expected performance in typical scenarios with random data.
  • Worst case: O(n²) - This happens when the pivot chosen is consistently the smallest or largest element, resulting in highly unbalanced partitions.

The space complexity is O(log n) for the recursive call stack in the best case, but can be O(n) in the worst case.

Despite the O(n²) worst-case scenario, quicksort is often faster in practice than other O(n log n) sorting algorithms like merge sort due to its cache efficiency and lower constant factors.
